Who we are

We are a team of five undergraduate students majoring in Computer Science at University of Massachusetts, Boston. This project helped bring us together and solidify our abilities to collaborate and work as team due to the challenge of the task at hand and the desire to make a tool that could benefit researchers working on this necessary field of study. Our goal was to make a successful scanning tool that will meet the needs and expectations of our clients in the University of Pennsylvania's Radiology Department. We designed this tool to help with the phenotyping of calcific atherosclerotic plaque deposits in the carotid artery, but this tool can also be used for other medical purposes as needed.

Our tool

CCS is a browser-based, semi-automatic tool that helps users identify morphologic features of carotid atherosclerotic calcifications. The current goal of this project is based around allowing users to identify areas of calcific plaque build-up and calculating morphologic features of the deposit based on the user annotation. Users will be able to use the brush tool to paint on the medical images and get calculations based on those annotations with a single click on the "Display Statistics" button. Users can use the Plus and Minus button to change the size of the brush to allow for more precise paint strokes or faster coverage of large areas. They also have the opportunity to pick between five colors listed in a drop-down menu for annotations: Red, Green, Blue, Purple, and Pink. There is a "Clear Brushes" button that clears all of brush markings on the displayed image. In case the user does not want to clear all annotations, there is an option to undo single brush strokes as well as a redo button that will return the brush stroke. Other available tools are the Zoom button, the Pan button, and the Length button. The Zoom tool allows users to magnify or reduce the image based around the location clicked on by the user, the Pan tool allows users to move their image around in the viewer, and the Length Tool enable users to take the distance between two points on the image.

How to use the scanner

Buttons Descriptions
Brush Tool: Allows users to paint on the diplayed DICOM image. When clicked, the Increment and Decrement of the brush size and the brush color drop-down menu are enabled. If you are a keyboard fan, you can press also press "b" to enable this tool.
Increase Brush size: Press on this to increase the size of your brush. If you are a keyboard fan, you can press also press "+" to enable this tool.
Decrease Brush size: Press on this to decrease the size of your brush. If you are a keyboard you can press also press "-" to enable this tool.
Brush-Color Press on this to open a drop-down menu and change the color of your brush. The color choices are red, green, purple, blue and pink. The default color for the brush is red.
Clear Brush Button: Press on this to clear any brush annotations on the DICOM image. If you are a keyboard fan, you can press also press "c" to enable this tool.
Undo Brush Button: Press on this to undo your brush strokes, starting with the most recently placed brushstroke. If you are a keyboard fan, you can press also press "ctrl + z" to enable this tool.
Redo Brush Button: Press on this to redo a brush stroke that you may have deleted by mistake. If you are a keyboard fan, you can press also press "ctrl + y" to enable this tool.
Zoom Button: Press on this to enable the Zoom tool. When clicked, the brush size, and the brush color buttons are disabled. To enable them again press on the brush button. If you are a keyboard fan, you can press also press "z" to enable this tool.
Pan Button: Press on this to allow movement of your image. Most useful when zoom is applied. If you are a keyboard fan, you can also press "p" to enable this tool.
Length Button: Press on this to take the length between two points placed by the user. If you are a keyboard fan, you can press also press "l" to enable this tool.
Display Statistics Button: Press on this to display the statistics calculated from the user's annotations to the right of the image. Those statistics include the discrete count of paint spots, the area delineated by the paint markings, the plaque density of that area based on Hounsfield Units and and the volume. These statistics being printed correspond to the matching paint color used on the DICOM. You can also press "s" to enable this tool.
Q Export JSON file: Press the key "d" on the keyboard to export the JSON file that will contain all the necessary annotations done on the DICOM image.

Clinic Relevance

Carotid artherosclerotic plaque in the carotid artery is a common cause of ischemic stroke in the brain. Atherosclerosis is the deposition of inflamed fat in the vessel walls that lead to hardening of the arteries. Hardened arteries with inflammation can result in unstable plaque, which can then break into pieces and travel to obstruct blood vessels in the brain. Lack of oxygenated blood flow can cause stroke, brain damage, and even death. Understanding plaque features that lead to instability is therefore absolutely critical for doctors to identify patients at risk for stroke. Calcific plaque is fascinating as it may have dual roles in both plaque stability and instability. This project involves helping researchers identify and characterize qualitative and quantitative morphologic features of calcific plaque. Using noninvasive diagnostic neuroimaging exams such as computed tomographic angiography (CTA) to image the blood vessels, users can identify patients with diseased vessels showing signs of atherosclerotic plaque buildup. Our client team includes neuroradiologists, vascular neurologists, and vascular surgeons at the University of Pennsylvania.
Scientific Definitions
Terms Definitions
CCS Carotid Calcium Scanner
DICOM Digital Imaging and Communications in Medicine. Type of input file used by CCS.
CT A Computed Tomography scan combines a series of X-ray images taken from different angles around your body and uses computer processing to create cross-sectional images (slices) of the bones, blood vessels, and soft tissues inside your body.
CTA Computed Tomography Angiography
PET Positron Emission Tomography scan
Plaque The buildup of fats, cholesterol, and other potentially harmful substances in and/or on the artery walls.
Atherosclerosis Thickening or hardening of the artery caused by a build up of plaque on the inner artery lining.
Calcifications Calcium that has built up in a body tissue, causing it to harden. Calcification of the arteries causes the arterial wall to stiffen and become inflamed, which can result in chunks of calcified plaque breaking off and obstructing blood flow to the brain.
Cerebrovascular Ischemic Event A term for when the flow of blood to the brain is reduced to a point where it cannot meet its metabolic demand. This leads to a reduction in oxygen flow to the brain, death of brain tissue, stroke, or death.
Stroke The sudden death of brain cells due to a lack of oxygen. The lack of oxygen is generally due to a blockage of blood flow or rupture of an artery connected to the brain.
Lumen The cavity or channel within a tube or tubular organ such as a blood vessel or the intestine.
Stenosis An abnormal narrowing of a channel in the body: in this case, referring to narrowing of the artery due to atherosclerosis.
